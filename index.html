Cybersecurity


What does it do?

Cybersecurity is defined as the state of being protected against the criminal or unauthorised use of electronic data, or the measures taken to achieve this. It is a rapidly growing field as technology as a whole becomes more advanced, and many jobs are in demand as large-scale companies need to ensure the safety of their sensitive data. Data protection is becoming more concerning as technology becomes more advanced, and some aim to use it for ill intentions. Currently, an extremely effective method of cybersecurity is known as 'two-factor authentication'. This method is so effective that it was published by the CIA for their cybersecurity awareness month. Two-factor authentication involves using a second device to authenticate who is really trying to log into the account. For example, if an individual is logging into an email account, two-factor authentication may send a text message to the owners phone, asking them to verify that it is not somebody else accessing their account. Overall cybersecurity is also becoming increasingly important to the everyday user. Issues with user privacy have resurfaced recently as many concerns have been raised about the security of large companies such as Facebook. Firewalls are also becoming incredibly more advanced as they must counteract the evolving threat of viruses such as malwares and spywares. Another instance of state-of-the-art cybersecurity is biometrics. Biometrics combine physical security with cybersecurity and although it is used in the physical world, it is still considered to be cybersecurity. Biometrics require a user to input unique physical data about themselves such as fingerprints or eye scans, and require them to be checked every time the data is accessed  Over the next few years cybersecurity can only be expected to continue to grow and develop as an IT field. More complex firewalls, new procedures, and leading technology companies will not halt development, and all users will become more secure. Antivirus developers such as AVG and Norton have not stopped updating their software since it's creation in order to keep up with the level of threats they face, and they are also becoming more readily available to the general public. As all areas of IT develop, so too will cybersecurity. In both hardware and software terms, all technology either urges cybersecurity to develop, or causes cybersecurity develop. New physical technology allows upgrades to existing systems such as bio metrics, processes between systems, and new secure technological storage, however advances in software can cause viruses to become more dangerous, and thus prompting the entire field of security to create a solution. As long as technology continues to evolve, so too will cybersecurity alongside it.


What is the likely impact?

The advancements in cyber security will only result in the general public's data being more secure than it was before. Major companies will not only be able to store their data safer, but also the data of their customers or users, so everyone will be impacted. It is likely that general, pre-installed security measures such as Windows Defender on Windows PC's will become more intelligent and more efficient at countering cyber threats as the technology continues to develop. As a result of this, the everyday user will likely be the most affected without them even realising it. As antivirus software and other processes become more commonplace across all digital platforms, they will become more protected without having to make an effort to protect themselves. As the importance of the field grows, users are unconsciously becoming more safe. In terms of jobs, some will become redundant as the technology is able to uphold it's own integrity, however, some will still be required in the event of an emergency. If the software solution fails, manual repairman or software engineers may be needed to repair the security measures. Overall, the impact of developing cybersecurity will be enormous across all of society. People will be able to store more of their identity online without the concern of being a victim to a cyber attack, companies will be able to further protect their confidential data about both customers and their own inner-workings, and everyday devices used by most people will be able to store their data without interference from third-party applications. The workforce will be partially disrupted as jobs won't be needed to reinforce the security of online storage and devices, but more specialised roles will be in demand to fix any complex problems that may occur.


How will this affect you?

My daily life, and the life of my friends and family will be not altered by the development in advanced cybersecurity in terms of how we use our devices. In recent times it has become evident that there are major companies who's cybersecurity measures can be defined as less than adequate. The amount of data leaks, data mines, and privacy breaches has increased over the past few years, and more complex cybersecurity will put a direct stop to it. Portable devices have become a focus for many cybersecurity experts due to a lot breaches spurring from how vulnerable they are. These changes will affect everyone without them realising it. Most users will not realise that constant software updates will be improving the integrity of their device's security, but security updates will allow for more sensitive information to reliably be stored digitally. Just as ideas such as Paypass or Apple Pay are only viable due to competent security measures, new procedures and technologies will be able to be reliably used as the technology develops. As stated in previously, users may often be impacted by the change without realising it. No action is often required by them to ensure their online safety as app updates and antivirus updates occur automatically, and companies change their privacy solutions and protocols without informing the user. Ultimately, although the life of a user will not change as they will still use their devices the same as before, it will be the small discreet changes that keep their privacy and data secure in the digital world

Project Ideas - Overtime

Summary:


Our project aims to make playing recreational sports a more social activity, and we have named it Overtime. The primary function of Overtime is to allow people to easily find public sport facilities and seamlessly meet up with friends at these locations. It will be a mobile app which will be functional on both Android and iOS systems. The initial home screen of Overtime will have a general map of the surrounding area which highlights all public soccer fields, football ovals, tennis courts, and basketball courts. This map can be zoomed in an out and scrolled across to view more of the surrounding area, functioning like any other mobile based mapping application. In the top left hand corner, the traditional 'hamburger' setting button will be visible to the user, with the side menu containing much of the apps functionality. Firstly, the 'Select a Sport' option will be on top of the list. This allows the user to filter out all unwanted fields and courts from their map view. For example, if the Basketball option is selected, then only basketball courts will be shown on the map. After selecting the sport, if the user does not wish to use the map view, they can select the "List view" button on the bottom of the screen to display nearby courts within a user-defined radius in order from closest to the furthest away. This sport option can be changed whenever the user pleases. Another tab within the side menu will be the "Friends" tab. This tab will allow users to add and manage a friends list based off a username they create when first downloading the app. Under the Friends tab will be the "Avatar" tab. This menu allows the user to create a virtual avatar of themselves with limited customisation of physical appearance, but a wide range of jerseys and team kits from many of the world's major sporting associations such as the EPL, NBA, and AFL. The defining function of Overtime will be the "Location" tab and will create a social aspect of the application. If a user is attending a court on a specific date, they can mark themselves as "going" or "playing" at that location. From the map view, friends of that user will be able to see that they are currently there, and the Friends tab allows you to view the set activity and location of all your friends. For privacy reasons, the app will not use real-time GPS tracking of anyone's device, but will rely on the user to mark themselves manually at a particular venue. Once a user is finished at the particular court or field, they can set themselves as "gone" which will remove their avatar from the map. Another option in the side menu will be the messages tab. This is fairly self-explanatory, as a user will be able to message any of their friends either through the map view or through the messages tab itself, allowing for people to meet up at whatever time or location they please. Finally, the last feature of Overtime will be the "Looking for Group" function. This is the last option in the side menu, and allows a user to mark themselves as interested in a particular sport at a specific venue. From this menu, a user will be able to see which of their friends are currently looking to play a sport or organize a team, and message them if they see fit. Ultimately, the purpose of Overtime is to make sports more social, and convince more people to get active with their friends. Through the various features within the app, we believe we have included enough ways for people to get into contact with each other and hopefully sports-lovers can convince their friends to adopt a healthier lifestyle, while removing the daunting perception of public sports venues. Playing a sport is a very fun pastime, and Overtime can make people believe that too.

Day In the Life of An I.T Professional

Day in the Life of an IT Professional (Technical Support Engineer)


1. What kind of work is done by the IT professional? The role of a technical support engineer is to ensure that staff members within the company and clients can operate their IT systems smoothly with minimal disruptions. They are required to commonly use problem solving skills for a variety of scenarios ranging from helping people with forgotten passwords, to  file and faulty hardware recovery.


2. What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with? Are they other IT professionals? Clients? Investors? The general public?  A Technical Support Engineer interacts with different people depending on the workplace. Some are hired for internal work and help other staff members with their IT troubles, where others work in a more client-based environment. If their role is to work with external clients, then they often help businesses both large and small to ensure the integrity and overall health of their IT system.


3. Where does the IT professional spend most of their time? Technical Support mostly involves spending time at an office or desk, however this is not always the case. An internal staff-focused position can sometimes require physical checks for hardware which requires the technical support engineer to go to the person they’re helping and inspect the issue in person.


4. What aspect of their position is most challenging?  One challenging issue that a Technical Support Engineer has to face is staff prioritisation. They must actively decide which issues are more important than others, and sort them based on urgency to ensure no significant damage is done to the company or client. These choices can greatly impact the efficiency of their own work and the work of others as they aim to fix any problems that are encountered.

Day in the Life of an IT Professional (Software Developer)


1. What kind of work is done by the IT professional? The role of a software developer can change drastically depending on the environment that they work in. However, no matter the workplace, they are responsible for creating software solutions to outstanding problems. They focus on one given task at a time, and use their time at work to code and develop a way to fix the issue. Often, they perform continuous work on one particular project such as a specific program or website and aim to add new features to it while ensuring it runs smoothly


2. What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with? Are they other IT professionals? Clients? Investors? The general public? Software developers mostly work with other people in the company that they work for. Along with providing general IT support to those who need it, they work in teams to brainstorm ideas for solutions and obtain tasks off managers and bosses. They do not work directly with the general public or users of their service, but they do check email complaints and work to fix any issues that clients have reportedly experienced


3. Where does the IT professional spend most of their time? Most of the time spent by a software developer is in the office, as they need a computer to fulfill their role. They can go and visit other staff members to discuss solutions and methods and fix other internal IT issues, but a majority of their time is spent at their own computers.


4. What aspect of their position is most challenging? A very challenging aspect of the software development position is simply creating solutions to extremely difficult problems. There can be days at work where they make no progress towards fixing the issue due to its complexity. Not all solutions are overly difficult, but the ones that can not be fixed can be very demoralising to the developer as they have an urge to obtain the satisfaction of finishing the solution.
